Tajmahal

The Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 for being “the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage”. It is regarded by many as the best example of Mughal architecture and a symbol of India's rich history.

Christ Redeemer Statue

The statue, made of reinforced concrete clad in a mosaic of thousands of triangular soapstone tiles, sits on a square stone pedestal base about 26 feet (8 metres) high, which itself is situated on a deck atop the mountain's summit. The statue is the largest Art Deco-style sculpture in the world.
